The spiritual center of Islamic learning and Sufi traditions in West Bengal

About Furfura Sharif

Furfura Sharif is a renowned Islamic spiritual center located in Hooghly district of West Bengal, India. It is the burial place of the prominent Sufi saint Hazrat Abu Bakr Siddique (R.A.) and his descendants.

Established in the 19th century, Furfura Sharif has been a beacon of Islamic spirituality, education, and social reform in the region, attracting devotees from all over India and beyond.

The center is known for its peaceful atmosphere, spiritual teachings, and contributions to Islamic scholarship and interfaith harmony.

Our History & Legacy

1829

Birth of Hazrat Abu Bakr Siddique (R.A.)

Hazrat Abu Bakr Siddique (R.A.), the founder of Furfura Sharif, was born in Furfura village. He would go on to become one of the most revered Islamic scholars and spiritual leaders of his time.

1873

Establishment of the Khanqah

The spiritual center (Khanqah) was formally established, becoming a hub for Islamic learning and Sufi practices. It attracted students and devotees from across Bengal.

1939

Passing of Hazrat Abu Bakr Siddique (R.A.)

After decades of spiritual leadership and social reform, Hazrat Abu Bakr Siddique (R.A.) passed away and was buried in Furfura Sharif. His mazar (shrine) became a place of pilgrimage.

1950s-60s

Expansion Under Successors

Under the leadership of his descendants, particularly Hazrat Shah Sufi Mohiuddin Siddique (R.A.), the center expanded its educational and charitable activities.

Present Day

Modern Spiritual Center

Today, Furfura Sharif continues to serve as an important center of Islamic spirituality in Eastern India, hosting annual Urs celebrations and providing religious guidance to millions.
